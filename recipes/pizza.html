Pizza recipe

Home Made Stone Oven Pizza

    Dough
  • All-purpose flour – 3 ½ cups (440 g)
  • Active dry yeast – 2 ¼ tsp (1 packet)
  • Warm water – 1 ¼ cups (300 ml)
  • Olive oil – 2 tbsp
  • Sugar – 1 tsp
  • Salt – 1 ½ tsp
    • Sauce
  • Crushed tomatoes – 1 can (14–15 oz / 400 g)
  • Garlic – 2 cloves, minced
  • Olive oil – 1–2 tbsp
  • Dried oregano – 1 tsp
  • Salt and pepper – to taste
    • Toppings
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese – 2 cups (200 g)
  • Optional toppings: pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, olives, fresh basil, or any favorite vegetables/meats
    • Optional finishing
  • Parmesan cheese – for sprinkling
  • Olive oil – drizzle before baking

Start by making the dough. In a large bowl, dissolve yeast and a pinch of sugar in warm water and let it sit until foamy. Stir in olive oil, salt, and flour gradually until a soft dough forms, then turn it out onto a floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise in a warm spot until doubled in size, about 1 to 1½ hours. While the dough rises, prepare a simple sauce by simmering crushed tomatoes with garlic, olive oil, salt, and dried oregano or basil for a few minutes.

Once the dough has risen, punch it down and stretch or roll it into a round on a floured surface. Transfer it to a baking sheet or pizza stone, spread a thin layer of sauce over the surface, and top with shredded mozzarella and any desired toppings such as pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, or olives. Bake in a very hot oven (475–500°F / 245–260°C) until the crust is golden and crisp and the cheese is bubbling and lightly browned. Let the pizza cool for a minute or two before slicing, then serve hot.
